Not just technology—
but human care

When I was younger, my grandpa used to tell me stories. But later, he gradually stopped hearing clearly—calling him for dinner took several tries, and at family gatherings he could only sit quietly and nod. When it came to hearing aids, he stubbornly refused: “If I wear that, isn’t it just admitting I’m old and hard of hearing?”

Watching him retreat into a lonely corner because he feared people’s judgment, I realized that 42% of people with hearing loss avoid wearing devices because they look too obvious. That pushed me to use my knowledge of acoustics and computer science to build something discreet and affordable—for my grandpa, and for countless workers who deserve to be heard.

“We aim to close the hearing-health gap for low-income communities in China—turning the ‘label’ of a hearing aid into the confidence of smart tech.”

— Aurix Team's Vision
